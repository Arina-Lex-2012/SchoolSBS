PRONUNCIATION

/h/, /aɪ/, and /iː/

/h/ H at the beginning of a word is pronounced /h/, e.g. hello. (There are a few exceptions, such as an hour.)

  • how

/aɪ/ This is actually a diphthong (literally ‘two sounds’). It is often spelled i before a single consonant followed by silent e, as in the example word bike.

  • like, nice, dice

/iː/ Two of the most common spellings of this sound are ee (double e), as in meet, and ea, as in repeat.

  • see, agree, screen
  • eat, seat, team, meat, feast, heat, eagel, season, speaker, reasonable
  • BUT break, weather, head

/ɪ/, /əʊ/, /s/, and /ʃ/

/ɪ/ The letter i between consonants is usually pronounced /ɪ/, e.g. fish. NB England (the e = /ɪ/) is irregular.
  • sit, fit, while
/əʊ/ In English, the sound of the letter o in phone is a diphthong (literally ‘two sounds’), i.e. a combination of the two sounds /ə/ + /ʊ/. It is usually spelled by the letter o or o + consonant + e.
